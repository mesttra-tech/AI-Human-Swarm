# **mesttra** AI Resiliency Plan

**mesttra** helps companies implement AI in a practical, secure, and operationally resilient way.

The core project behind AI-Human-Swarm is the **AI Resiliency Plan**: a defensive operating model that helps companies connect AI agents and MCP-enabled workflows to their environment while monitoring cyber readiness, infrastructure operations, dependency risk, automation governance, and AI adoption maturity.

Public context: **mesttra** presents itself through technology education, mentoring, digital inclusion, talent development, impact-company programs, and connection between people and technology opportunities. This project turns that practical learning model toward AI implementation and AI resilience.

Public source: <https://www.mesttra.com>

## What The AI Resiliency Plan Helps Companies Do

- Connect AI agents to company workflows through MCP and controlled tool access.
- Monitor cyber readiness across repositories, dependencies, CI/CD, credentials, and developer tooling.
- Review infrastructure and operations readiness before AI automation expands.
- Identify unsafe agent behaviors, over-permissioned tools, and weak approval gates.
- Turn threat intelligence into practical daily patches and team playbooks.
- Build internal AI capability through education, mentoring, implementation support, and repeatable practice.

## Why This Matters

Companies are adopting AI faster than their governance, security, and operations practices are evolving.

The risk is not only AI hallucination. The larger operational risk is connecting AI to real tools, data, credentials, infrastructure, package managers, CI/CD, and business workflows without enough visibility or control.

The **AI Resiliency Plan** helps companies move from AI experimentation to AI operations with safer defaults.

## How **mesttra** Can Help

### AI Resiliency Plan

A practical readiness and monitoring program for teams that want to connect AI to real work without losing control of security, operations, and governance.

### MCP-Based Monitoring

Guidance for connecting MCP-enabled workflows to company systems with controlled permissions, human approval gates, and reviewable tool behavior.

### Cyber Readiness Review

Assessment of repositories, package managers, dependencies, CI/CD, credentials, agent configuration, and public-safety practices.

### Infrastructure Operations Readiness

Review of the operational surfaces that AI automation may touch, including deployment workflows, observability, incident response, access control, and change management.

### AI Implementation Programs

Structured programs to help teams identify use cases, build prototypes, document workflows, and move toward production-ready AI adoption.

### AI Talent Development

Education and mentoring so teams learn how to work with AI systems, evaluate outputs, define safe workflows, and operate with human accountability.

## How Companies Can Engage

Companies can express interest without scheduling a call first.

Open a GitHub issue using the **AI Resiliency Plan interest** form. The form helps **mesttra** understand:

- The company website and public company context.
- Approximate company size.
- The person contacting the project and their decision role.
- AI goals and current AI maturity.
- Main cyber, infrastructure, and operations risks.
- Existing systems that may need integration.
- Whether the company is interested in the full **AI Resiliency Plan**, MCP-based monitoring, cyber readiness, infrastructure operations readiness, AI implementation, or AI talent development.
- The preferred async next step.

Please do not include confidential details, credentials, private logs, customer data, contracts, private architecture, internal URLs, or sensitive operational details in the issue.

## Engagement Path

1. Read [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).
2. Open an **AI Resiliency Plan interest** issue.
3. Include the company website so maintainers can review public company context and estimate size from public sources.
4. Choose the decision role that best describes the person contacting the project.
5. Select the risk areas and systems involved using categories only.
6. Wait for a maintainer to reply with a suggested path, checklist, or readiness starting point.

## Boundary

This repository is public. Keep every issue and pull request generic, defensive, and safe for anyone to read.

Do not include secret values, private company information, sensitive security findings, customer data, non-public architecture, internal-only context, or anything that would require a private channel to discuss safely.
